2It all comes down to who wins the Cup

The champion is worth 100 points, so the trophy is the master switch. Sort the contenders by who lifts it and most of the pool decides itself:

🇪🇸 Spain wins the Cup → soffffffff wins the pool. Lock — the only Spain-backer left.
🇧🇪 Belgium wins the Cup → Saka Rice Krispies wins. Lock — Belgium is his finalist and nobody else has them deep.
🇫🇷 France wins the Cup → channmann or White-Morpheus, decided by 🇳🇴 Norway–England 🏴 (Norway → channmann, England → White-Morpheus). They both picked France as champion, so it drops to that one difference.
🇦🇷 Argentina wins the Cup → usually Saka, but it opens up to soffffffff, Hitenpatel11 and iamphilco if 🇪🇸 Spain beats Belgium 🇧🇪.
🎲 A team nobody crowned wins (Morocco, Switzerland, Norway, England) → back to the co-leaders, Saka vs soffffffff, decided by 🇪🇸 Spain–Belgium 🇧🇪.

3The three games that decide everything

  • The Final
    Who wins the Cup — the 100-point master switch. 🇪🇸 Spain → soffffffff, 🇧🇪 Belgium / 🇦🇷 Argentina → Saka-ish, 🇫🇷 France → the channmann/White-Morpheus duel.
  • 🇪🇸 Spain v Belgium 🇧🇪 — Quarterfinal (Fri)
    The Saka-vs-soffffffff swing. Belgium going deep tilts it to Saka; Spain going deep tilts it to soffffffff. This one shadows almost every branch.
  • 🇳🇴 Norway v England 🏴 — Quarterfinal (Sat)
    The channmann-vs-White-Morpheus swing — live only if France go all the way.

4The quirk: you don't strictly need to pick the winner

Getting the final matchup right is almost as valuable as picking the champion. Two correct finalists are worth +150; the champion topper is only +100 on top of that.

So a co-leader who nailed the exact two finalists can still win the pool even if their champion loses the final — as long as both of those teams actually get there. Case in point: if 🇪🇸 Spain and 🇦🇷 Argentina both reach the final, soffffffff wins no matter who lifts the trophy (that's her exact pairing) — because she still banks Argentina as a finalist and only forfeits the 100-point topper, and her pinpoint accuracy everywhere else covers the gap.

5Why so many are already out — the "similar bracket" curse

Most of the eliminated didn't get unlucky — they got copied and outscored. If someone sits ahead of you and still has every team you have alive, plus the same champion, they match or beat you in every remaining game. You can only ever tie them, never pass them — a strictly-worse version of their bracket. A handful of others weren't beaten by any one person; the whole field just quietly climbed over them. Either way: no path to first.
