4The quirk: you don't strictly need to pick the winner

Getting the final matchup right is almost as valuable as picking the champion. Two correct finalists are worth +150; the champion topper is only +100 on top of that.

So a co-leader who nailed the exact two finalists can still win the pool even if their champion loses the final — as long as both of those teams actually get there. Case in point: if 🇪🇸 Spain and 🇦🇷 Argentina both reach the final, soffffffff wins no matter who lifts the trophy (that's her exact pairing) — because she still banks Argentina as a finalist and only forfeits the 100-point topper, and her pinpoint accuracy everywhere else covers the gap.

5Why so many are already out — the "similar bracket" curse

Most of the eliminated didn't get unlucky — they got copied and outscored. If someone sits ahead of you and still has every team you have alive, plus the same champion, they match or beat you in every remaining game. You can only ever tie them, never pass them — a strictly-worse version of their bracket. A handful of others weren't beaten by any one person; the whole field just quietly climbed over them. Either way: no path to first.
